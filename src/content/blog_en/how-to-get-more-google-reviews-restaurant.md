Google reviews have become the digital storefront of your business. 87% of customers check them before walking through your door. Yet most business owners struggle to collect them consistently — without seeming desperate or risking negative reviews.

Here's the method we've tested with over 100 Grow Lot partner merchants.

## Why Recent Reviews Matter More Than Volume

Google Maps prioritizes businesses with **recent** reviews. The local algorithm rewards consistency: a restaurant with 30 reviews this month will often outrank a competitor with 300 reviews from 2 years ago.

> A review posted yesterday is worth 10x more than one posted 6 months ago for your local ranking.

That's why you need to turn review collection into a habit, not a one-off campaign.

## The 3 Mistakes That Kill Your Review Collection

**1. Asking at the wrong time**
An unhappy customer, too long a wait, a botched order — that's not the moment. The review request should come after a peak satisfaction moment.

**2. Asking without making it easy**
"Don't forget to leave us a review" → conversion rate: near zero. The customer should be able to leave a review in under 20 seconds.

**3. Sending everyone to Google**
This is the classic trap. Without a filter, your most dissatisfied customers are often the most motivated to write. Result: your rating drops.

## How to Respond to Reviews to Maximize Impact

Responding to reviews — positive and negative — is a strong signal for Google. Here's the formula that works:

**Positive review**: thank by name, mention a personalized detail, invite them back.

**Negative review**:
1. Thank them for the feedback
2. Apologize without justifying yourself
3. Offer a concrete offline solution
4. Stay short and professional

Never a defensive response. Never a lengthy explanation. Future customers read your responses as much as the reviews themselves.
